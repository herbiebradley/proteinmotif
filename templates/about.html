About

Amyloidosis is a group of diseases characterised by build up of amyloids – aggregates of proteins. These aggregates are caused by misfolded proteins which form beta sheet structures that accumulate together into long fibres – the amyloids (Paz and Serrano, 2004). Amyloidosis encompasses a wide range of diseases, such as rheumatoid arthritis and type II diabetes, but particularly neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease.

One active area of research is the link between a protein sequence and the protein’s aggregation propensity, and various tools have been created to identify the parts of a protein sequence which are particularly prone to aggregation (Conchillo-Solé et al., 2007; Ahmed and Kajava, 2013).

Paz and Serrano (2004) identified the following six-residue PROSITE pattern indicating an amyloid-prone fragment in neutral pH:

{P}-{PKRHW}-[VLSWFNQ]-[ILTYWFN]-[FIY]-{PKRH}

Protein Amyloid Motif Search (PAMS) is a web-based tool designed to search a protein sequence for matches of this amyloid motif pattern.
